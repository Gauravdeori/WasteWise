The Hidden Truth

Food Wastage in India — From Farm to Bin

Behind every plate left unfinished lies a chain of wasted water, energy, labour and money. The numbers tell a story we can no longer ignore.

Household Wastage

According to the UNEP's Food Waste Index report, 68.7 million tonnes of food is wasted annually in Indian homes — about 50 kg per person. India stands 2nd worldwide in household food wastage, only after China.

A Global Crisis

The National Resources Defense Council (NRDC) reports that 40% of food produced goes uneaten in the US. In Asia, approximately 1.34 billion tonnes of food is wasted — the main contributors being India and China.

Economic Loss

In India, the food wasted is equivalent to nearly ₹89,000 crore per year — almost 1% of the country's GDP depleted in the form of food wastage, from farm to bin.

"Studies show that businesses, such as hotels, restaurants and food retailers, earn financial returns by investing in food waste reduction."

— The Guardian

Who We Are

About the Project

India stands 2nd worldwide in household food wastage (UNEP Food Waste Index). We are building the technology to change that — starting from institutional kitchens and hostels.

Why WasteWise Exists

Every day, institutional kitchens across India prepare thousands of meals — and a significant share of that food never gets eaten. Without measurement, this waste stays invisible: nobody knows how much is lost, what it costs, or where to intervene.

WasteWise was born at the TIDF Research Building, IIT Guwahati, as an IoT-driven answer to this problem. By placing smart, connected weighing scales at the point where waste is generated, we convert every discarded plate into a data point — and every data point into an opportunity to save food, money and the environment.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the Waste Scale work?

The Waste Scale is a load-cell based weighing platform connected to a Wi-Fi enabled microcontroller. Whenever food waste is placed on it, the weight is recorded automatically and transmitted to the cloud — no manual entry needed.

Can WasteWise be installed in an existing kitchen or mess?

Yes. The system is designed to fit into existing workflows — the scale simply replaces the regular waste bin stand, and the dashboard can be accessed from any browser.

What kind of insights does the dashboard provide?

The dashboard shows the amount, estimated monetary value and carbon footprint of waste in real time, along with daily, weekly and monthly trends, category-wise breakdowns and progress against reduction targets.

Use the form above or write to us at foodwatch@iitg.ac.in — we're actively looking for partner institutions for pilot programs.
